Three Approaches to the Build Analyzer

Each builds on the previous. Which scope feels right?

A

Navigation Fixes Only

Fix the two core frustrations: zoom anchors to the visible center (no more losing your place), and minimap click centers the viewport on the clicked position instead of jumping to the leading edge. Minimal scope, high-confidence win.

  • ✅ Anchor-preserving zoom
  • ✅ Minimap centers on click
  • ✅ Minimap drag moves viewport smoothly
  • ❌ No label or hierarchy improvements
  • ❌ No new insights
B

Navigation + Readability — Recommended

Everything in A, plus a cohesive pass on visual clarity: hierarchy connector lines, richer hover tooltips showing timing and wait context, label fade-out instead of hard cutoff, idle gap shading, and a smarter minimap that shows critical path in gold.

  • ✅ Everything in A
  • ✅ Connector lines between parent/child rows
  • ✅ Idle gap shading (dead time between processes)
  • ✅ Rich hover tooltip (timing, category, wait time)
  • ✅ Minimap shows critical path in gold
  • ❌ No new summary panel
C

Full Experience Redesign

Everything in B, plus a Build Health overview panel docked above the timeline: key metrics at a glance (critical path duration, top bottlenecks, parallelism grade), with clickable hotspots that jump directly to the relevant region.

  • ✅ Everything in B
  • ✅ Build Health panel (top bottlenecks, critical path summary)
  • ✅ Clickable insight hotspots → scroll to node
  • ✅ Keyboard navigation (←/→ arrows between critical path nodes)
  • ⚠️ Larger scope, more risk

My recommendation: B. It addresses every pain you mentioned (navigation, labels, hierarchy, insights) without the scope risk of a full panel redesign. C can always be a follow-up.
